Ancient History of Greece

Ancient Greece was one of the most influential civilizations in world history. Located in the southeastern part of Europe, ancient Greece is known for its advancements in various fields such as philosophy, art, politics, and science. This civilization began around the 8th century BC and lasted until the fall of the Byzantine Empire in the 15th century AD.

Greek Mythology

Greek mythology is full of gods, goddesses, and mythological creatures that have become an important part of their cultural heritage. Some famous figures include Zeus, the god of the sky, Hera, the goddess of marriage, and Athena, the goddess of wisdom. The stories about these gods are not only entertaining but also illustrate the moral and spiritual beliefs of the ancient Greeks.

Greek Influence in the Modern World

The legacy of ancient Greece can still be found throughout the modern world, from the democratic system inspired by Athens to the Olympic Games first held in Olympia. In addition, many works of Greek art, architecture and philosophy are still studied and appreciated today.
